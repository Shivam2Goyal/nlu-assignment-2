Observation:
  Both models capture meaningful semantic relationships from the
  IIT Jodhpur corpus.  Skip-Gram with Negative Sampling typically
  produces more semantically coherent neighbors, especially for
  less frequent words, because it optimises each (center, context)
  pair individually -- giving rare words more training signal.
  CBOW tends to average context, which can blur distinctions for
  rarer terms but often yields strong results for frequent words.
